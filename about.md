    <p>Hi! My name is Nirali Somia and I'm currently a first year PhD student in the Medical Engineering and Medical Physics (<a href="https://hst.mit.edu/academic-programs/memp"><u>MEMP</u></a>) program through Health Sciences and Technology (HST) at Harvard and MIT. I am coadvised by <a href="https://lotterlab.github.io/"><u>Dr. Bill Lotter</u></a> and <a href="https://zaklab.org/"><u>Dr. Isaac Kohane</u></a>. I'm currently interested in AI interpretability, evaluation, and alignment in healthcare.</p>

    <p>My research journey started in highschool with Dr. Lihsia Chen at the University of Minnesota. I worked with C. Elegans and, though I'm completely dry lab now, I will always have a bit of a soft spot for those worms. I received my B.S. in computer science and minors in biology and writing from <a href="https://washu.edu/"><u>Washington University in St. Louis</u></a>. During college I did the summer research program through NYU's Center for Data Science and worked with <a href="https://gracewlindsay.com/"><u>Dr. Grace Lindsay</u></a>. After I graduated from WashU I was a PREP scholar at <a href="https://www.cshl.edu/"><u>Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory</u></a> in <a href="https://koo-lab.github.io/"><u>Dr. Peter Koo's</u></a> lab.</p>

    <p>I mostly started this website to practice making difficult concepts more accessible by writing blogs about them! I also wanted to just generally force myself to write more. You can find some of those posts <a href="https://niralisomia.com/blog/"><u>here</u></a>.</p>

    <p>Overall, I think the whole point of life is to learn and to love; I feel very lucky to get to do just that day after day. Outside of science I like to read fiction and poetry, crochet, rock climb, and listen to music. Some of my favorite poets and authors are <a href="https://allpoetry.com/poem/15374223-Wild-geese-by-Mary-J-Oliver"><u>Mary Oliver</u></a>, <a href="https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poems/1618980/the-end-of-poetry"><u>Ada Limon</u></a>, and <a href="https://poetryinvoice.ca/read/poems/someday-ill-love-ocean-vuong"><u>Ocean Vuong</u></a>! If you'd like to learn more about what I enjoy reading, watching, and listening to, I wrote a little bit about that <a href="https://niralisomia.com/blog/2026/04/28/Some_Things_I_Enjoy/"><u>here</u></a> :) </p>
